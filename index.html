ABOUT ME

Sung Won Caleb Bhyun

Born in South Korea, I moved to Canada with my family during middle school. Growing up, I was always interested in the world of computers. In my teenage years, despite knowing nothing about programming, I ran a game server and a website, getting spoon-fed by people on online forums while figuring things out along the way.


After high school, I started university in General Science, unsure of which path to take. That changed when I took a Computer Science course as an elective. It felt like the right fit, and I switched my degree program.


As a South Korean citizen, military service was mandatory. I joined the Republic of Korea Navy and was fortunate to be selected for the Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT), where I gained hands-on experience monitoring systems and networks, responding to incidents, and managing infrastructure. That is where my interest in cyber security began.


After successfully completing my service and earning my degree, I started out as a software developer, as breaking into the cyber security industry as an international student seemed out of reach. That changed when an opportunity came along as a security analyst, then I was focusing on defending organizations from cyberattacks.


Today, I work on the offensive side as an ethical hacker, finding vulnerabilities and misconfigurations, demonstrating their impact, and providing solutions for remediation.


I have since become a Canadian citizen and, on the side, joined the Canadian Army Reserve as a part-time member to give back to my new country and build on the military experience I had come to value.


Outside of work, I am always looking for new experiences. I love travelling, meeting new people, exploring different cultures, and trying new foods!
